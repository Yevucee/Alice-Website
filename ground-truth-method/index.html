Traditional research can tell you what is documented, reported or formally presented. It may not reveal how decisions are really made, who holds influence without formal authority, where official accounts differ from lived reality, how people behave outside formal meetings, which tensions are visible locally but absent from reports, or whether a strategy, investment or organisation works as described. The method

The Alice Ground Truth Method

The Alice Ground Truth Method is our proprietary approach to understanding complex environments through people, relationships, observation and strategic interpretation. It combines structured research, network-led field movement, natural human-first conversation, direct observation, stakeholder and influence mapping, continuous testing of assumptions, and senior-level synthesis. We do not rely on one interview, one source or one official narrative. We build understanding progressively, moving across networks, comparing perspectives and observing how systems behave in practice.

We do not only research an environment. We gradually become present within it.

The sequence and the internal frameworks for access, observation, verification and interpretation are proprietary to Alice.

The sequence

How the method works

  1. 01

    Orient

    We begin with the decision behind the assignment, and identify what is known, what is assumed, what is missing and what needs closer attention.

  2. 02

    Enter

    We enter the environment through people, relationships and credible local access, creating the conditions for genuine contact and observation rather than a rigid sequence of formal interviews.

  3. 03

    Move

    One conversation leads to another. We move across stakeholder groups, organisations, social settings and informal networks, building a broader picture of the environment.

  4. 04

    Test

    We compare what people say, what others say, what we observe and what the records show, and we test emerging conclusions continuously rather than accepting them too early.

  5. 05

    Interpret

    We translate fragmented information into clear judgement, explaining the patterns, relationships, risks and implications that matter to the decision.

Why it is different

Research built around human reality

Human-first engagement

+

We create conversations in which people speak naturally, which often reveals more than rigid or formal questioning.

In practice, this means meeting people where they are comfortable, and listening for what is said in passing as much as what is said on the record.

Network-led access

+

Our work develops through relationships and introductions, reaching people and perspectives that rarely appear on a conventional stakeholder list.

Access is earned, not requested. One trusted introduction opens the next, and the map of who matters grows from the inside.

Social agility

+

We move comfortably between senior leadership, employees, advisers, operational teams, local communities and informal networks.

The same situation looks different from the boardroom and the factory floor. We spend time in both.

Observation beyond the meeting room

+

We attend to behaviour, routines, surroundings, language and the details that are easily overlooked.

How a place is run is often written in its habits long before it appears in its reports.

Multiple perspectives

+

No single account is treated as definitive. We compare perspectives and find where experiences, interests and narratives align or diverge.

Where accounts diverge is often where the most useful understanding begins.

Strategic interpretation

+

Field insight matters when it is connected to the client's decision. We turn observation into structured judgement, not description.

The question is never only what is happening, but what it means for the choice in front of you.
